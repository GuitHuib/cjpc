The Criminal Justice Policy Coalition

The Criminal Justice Policy Coalition (CJPC) was created in 1996 in order to bring together those individuals and organizations concerned about the individual and societal repercussions of the increasingly retributive nature of criminal justice policy in Massachusetts.

What is CJPC?

CJPC is dedicated to improving our community through the advancement of effective, just, and humane criminal justice policy in Massachusetts. In addition to our advocacy on many issues, we are proud to have subcommittees focused on these issues.

Sex Offender Policy Reform Initiative (SOPRI) is a group of concerned citizens working for more rational evidence based practices to deal with people convicted of sexual crimes. They work to secure better legislation through education and demystifying the social stigma of Registrants.

Parole Review for All works with other community groups and organizations focusing on ending all life without parole sentences in all contexts and for any crime.
